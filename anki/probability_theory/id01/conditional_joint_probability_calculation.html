How can a conditional joint probability be calculated?
Remember for joint probabilites: $$ P(A,B) = P(A|B) \cdot P(B) = P(B|A) \cdot P(A) $$ Thus, the conditional joint probability is $$ \fbox{$P(A,B|C) = P(A|B,C) \cdot P(B|C) = P(B|A,C) \cdot P(A|C)$} $$ Any probability result that is true for unconditional probability remains true if everything is conditioned on some event.
